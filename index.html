01 · Why This Guide Was Built

Built for
Decision Makers

This guide was developed to better leverage the market intelligence at our disposal and equip the SLT and XLT with competitive data in a digestible, navigable format - enabling more informed broker conversations, more tactical risk selection and pricing decisions, and better-disciplined underwriting across our portfolio, lines, and regions.

⛭
Underwriting Leaders
Risk selection • Pricing • Broker conversations
  • Broker renewal prep: Checking which peers are growing a specific LOB and at what COR can indicate how aggressively the market may be competing for the same risks before a negotiation.
  • New business appetite: LOBs where few competitors appear to be growing may signal limited competition and potential opportunity to write selectively at favourable terms.
  • Portfolio rebalancing: The LOB scatter plot can help identify which lines sit in the high-premium, low-COR quadrant across peers. Regional pages can then offer a view of provinces with room to grow.
  • Rate adequacy benchmarking: Comparing LM Canada's growth rate against peers on the same line may surface whether pricing is above or below where the market appears to be settling.
  • Competitor strategy detection: The Top 3 by Growth table P10 can help spot carriers making deliberate multi-year plays into a line, and at what scale.
⚖
Claims Leaders
Reserving context • Market loss trends • CAT benchmarking
  • Reserve review context: Checking peer group loss ratios on the same LOB over six periods can help validate whether a deteriorating NLR is LM Canada-specific or a market-wide trend.
  • Emerging loss trend detection: When incurred claims bars widen relative to revenue bars across the peer set on the Profitability Trend chart, it may indicate systemic severity pressure across the market.
  • CAT proportionality check: After a major loss event, comparing LM Canada's provincial market share against its share of industry claims can offer a view of whether losses appear proportionate to exposure.
  • Claims benchmarking by LOB: Sorting the Peer Insights table by Incurred Claims or NLR per LOB can help identify where LM Canada's claims experience may be an outlier versus where it tracks the market.
  • Supporting rate change requests: Industry-wide claims deterioration on a line may provide useful external evidence alongside internal data when making the case for rate changes.

02 · Start Here

Before You
Open a Page

This guide covers the Executive Summary - a focused subset of the broader Competitors Analysis Insights Playbook. The full Playbook contains additional reports including the Comp Data - SBU Focused report. The Executive Summary is structured across 13 report pages grouped into six dashboards: Growth, Peer Group, Profitability, LOB, Regional, and Company Insights. All analysis is strictly focused on commercial lines that LM Canada [Branch] writes. Personal lines (personal property, personal auto) and certain commercial lines such as Accident & Sickness are excluded from other carriers' totals to keep comparisons on a level playing field.

What the data represents
All numbers are sourced from MSA Research, which compiles and normalises OSFI statutory filings from every federally registered P&C carrier in Canada. What you see is what each carrier reported to OSFI. No internal adjustments or GRS allocations are included. Competitors' data is prepared in accordance with International Financial Reporting Standards as submitted to OSFI. Note that competitors may use a different basis of preparation for their own management reporting.

Because the data is sourced from MSA, it is limited to what competitors have formally filed with OSFI. If a peer does not report certain U.S. business as “In Canada” business, that activity will not appear in their dataset. All figures presented here reflect filed results only.
IFRS 17 Note
From 2023 onward, all carriers report under IFRS 17. Top-line metrics (GWP, DWP) are not materially affected. Bottom-line metrics (COR, NLR) have been restated on an IFRS 17 basis throughout so comparisons across all years remain valid. For example, what was previously called Claims Incurred under IFRS 4 is now Insurance Service Expense under IFRS 17 - the economic substance is the same.

Branch vs SBU: What You Need to Know

LM Canada [Branch] is our official legal entity as filed with OSFI. Since every competitor also files as a single legal entity, Branch is the apples-to-apples basis for all peer and industry comparisons in this report. Unless a visual is explicitly labelled SBU, all LM Canada numbers are Branch.

LM Canada [SBU] is our internal management view - the results presented in quarterly performance updates. SBU figures are included for context only and should not be used to benchmark against competitors, as they are not prepared on the same basis as OSFI filings.

Bottom line: When comparing LM Canada to the market, use Branch. SBU is for internal reference only, providing additional context behind items that are reported differently at the management level versus the regulatory level. See the Key Differences section below for a full breakdown.

Key Differences & Notes: Branch vs SBU
Components of Business
Branch Key Differences
  • + Surety
  • + Bill C-80 products (cross-border transactions)
  • − Workers Compensation (excluded)
  • ⓘ Property under OSFI terminology includes commercial property, package, energy and construction lines
  • ⓘ EIL business is defined as Pollution under OSFI lines
SBU Key Differences
  • + Workers Compensation
  • − Surety (excluded)
  • − Bill C-80 products (excluded)
  • − Global programs (excluded; note: other insurers' global programs are included in their totals)
Programs & LOB Structure

Both Branch and SBU include Programs business. However Programs cannot be broken out separately because all industry data follows OSFI's required LOB format - and we do not have external data to remove programs from industry analysis. To be comparable to industry, programs data is left in for both SBU and Branch results.

Similarly, ARS sits within the individual LOBs in this report rather than appearing as a standalone line, consistent with Industry Data. A dedicated analysis of ARS impact is available in the separate Comp Data - SBU Focused report.

Other Technical Differences
  • Actuarial reserves:Branch (OSFI) applies discounting and risk margin factors per LOB. SBU does not apply these adjustments.
  • Internal reinsurance:Reinsurance purchased from Liberty's global reinsurance programme is treated as notional reinsurance under OSFI reporting and is not recognised the same way in SBU.
  • LOB groupings:OSFI specifies how all registered insurers report by class of business. These groupings may differ from lines used internally (e.g., MLOB). This affects how certain products appear when comparing Branch to SBU at the LOB level.

04 · Navigation Basics

How to Move
Through the Report

Every page uses the same navigation system. The Insights Panel appears in the top-right area of every page. All slicers listed beneath it are page-level and affect every visual on the page simultaneously.

← → Arrow buttons
Move one page forward or backward in the report. The quickest way to browse page by page without going back to the main menu.
Clear button
Reset all filters on the current page to defaults. Use this whenever a visual looks unexpected or a filter from a previous selection is still active. Always clear before starting a fresh analysis on any page.
Search within a slicer
Click any dropdown slicer (Company, Period) and a search box appears at the top of the list. Type the company name or quarter to find it instantly without scrolling.

Universal Filter Rules

  • 1
    Default period is always the most recent date in the dataset. You do not need to set this unless you want to view a historical period.
  • 2
    Multi-select: hold Ctrl and click to select more than one item in a slicer. For example, hold Ctrl to select both GRS NA Peers and LM Canada Peers at the same time.
  • 3
    Cross-filtering: clicking any bar, segment, or bubble in a chart filters all other visuals on the page. Click the same element again to deselect and restore all data.
  • 4
    Tooltips: hover over any data point, bar, bubble, or segment to see a pop-up with detailed values. On scatter plots this is essential as bubbles can overlap.
  • 5
    Sort tables: click any column header arrow in a data table to sort by that metric ascending or descending.

Why Liberty [Branch] and Liberty [SBU] Are Their Own Peer Group

In the Peer Group filter, you will notice that Liberty [Branch] and Liberty [SBU] appear as selectable peer groups in their own right, separate from the Tier I and Tier II groupings.

This is deliberate. LM Canada [Branch] and LM Canada [SBU] are the same legal entity but represent two different views of the business. If they were placed inside Tier I or Tier II, their numbers would be counted inside the tier average and would distort the peer group benchmark that LM Canada is comparing itself against.

By keeping Liberty [Branch] and Liberty [SBU] as standalone selectable peer groups, you can:

  • ▶Select LM Canada Peers to see the Tier II benchmark and compare LM Canada against it without LM Canada polluting its own benchmark
  • ▶Select Liberty [Branch] to isolate only LM Canada Branch data across all visuals
  • ▶Select multiple groups using Ctrl+click to compare Branch alongside a peer tier simultaneously

CAGR: GWP Performance Table
Dynamic • Responds to all page filters
This is the centrepiece of the page. Each row is a company. Each column is a reporting quarter. The value in each cell is the Compound Annual Growth Rate (CAGR) looking backward by the number of years you selected.

What is CAGR? CAGR smooths out year-to-year volatility and tells you the steady annual rate a company would need to grow to get from its starting value to its ending value. It eliminates one-year spikes and gives a fair view of sustained momentum.

How the table behaves dynamically: If you select a peer group, only those companies appear. If you change the CAGR filter to, say, five years, every cell recalculates to show the five-year compound growth at each quarter. Scroll right to see the full history. Negative values mean the company shrank over that period.

Top Growing Companies (% view vs Volume view)
The most important toggle on this page
Percentage growth is misleading without context. A carrier writing a small premium base that doubles shows a very high growth rate. A carrier writing a much larger base that grows by a similar dollar amount shows a modest percentage. The percentage view correctly captures the rate of change but overstates the competitive significance of small carriers.

Why volume view matters: Switching to volume view shows who is actually moving the most dollars of premium. A carrier growing by a large dollar amount is making a real competitive impact on LM Canada's market position - regardless of their percentage rate. Use percentage to spot emerging challengers and volume to understand who is truly dominating the province.

Performance Summary Report Card
The centrepiece of the Company Insights page
A structured table comparing the selected company across eight metrics and five dimensions. Here is how to read each column:

Results: The selected company's own value for the selected period.

Vs. Liberty: Shows an arrow and LM Canada [Branch]'s number for the same metric. A green upward arrow means the selected company is outperforming Liberty on that metric. A red downward arrow means underperforming. Direction is defined correctly per metric: lower is better for COR and NLR; higher is better for growth metrics.

Vs. Peer Group: The selected company compared against its peer group aggregate for that metric.

Vs. Industry: Compared against the full industry average for that metric.

Industry Rank: The selected company's ordinal rank in the full industry on that metric. First is the best-performing carrier in the market.

The eight metrics are: DWP YoY Growth, GWP YoY Growth, NLR, COR, Average NLR (3 Years), Average NLR (5 Years), Average COR (3 Years), and Average COR (5 Years). Multi-year averages smooth single-year anomalies and reveal sustained profitability track record.
How to use the Report Card in a meeting
Select LM Canada [Branch] to see the Branch's full scorecard on all eight metrics in one view. The arrows immediately show where Branch outperforms and underperforms vs Liberty, peers, and industry. The Industry Rank column provides precise market positioning context. Use the multi-year averages to show that a single strong or weak quarter does not define the company's profitability trend.
